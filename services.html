Services

Introduction

Nipige is built upon microservice architecture where services are grouped in 5 categories

  • Primary Services

  • Helper Services

  • Background Services

  • Third Party Services

  • Databases (Persisted/Cache)

Primary Services

There are many primary microservices which power nipige logic, some of the primary ones are RBAC, CAP, Catalog, Storage, Search.

Read More

Helper Services

Within nipige there are many services which perform basic task which power primary nipige services but aren't directly involved in user facing system. These kind of services manage work like sync, batch processing, rabbit mq events handling, elastic search sync etc.

Read More

Background Services

These services indirectly power nipige system with no dependency on primary or helper services. Their primary job is to perform defined set of tasks at their specific configurations. One of such service is mongo-elasticsearch-sync whose primary job is to sync items collection from mongodb to elasticsearch during creation and update.

Read More

Third Party Services

There are the services which are managed by other providers or deployed as it is on our servers, redis, netflix-conductor, kafka, postgres, mongodb, rabbit-mq, influx
